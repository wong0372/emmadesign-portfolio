Case Study - Travel Apps

Background

I worked with a group of three students who are passionate about pursuing careers in UX design, and we recently conducted a case study on travel apps. Our team made a case study of travel app named "TravelGigs," designed specifically for individuals with permits to work while traveling. Our aim was to create an app that addresses the unique needs of this niche user group.

The Problem

Different countries have various regulations for working while traveling, such as working holiday visas that permit travelers to work or exchange services. However, finding a suitable workspace can be challenging, and information may be scattered, especially for unique job types like cultural exchange positions.

The Solution

The app could aggregate all types of job opportunities offered by hosts worldwide, allowing users to find diverse job options in one place. Additionally, users could make friends, leave reviews for each job, and share their travel experiences on the appâ€™s platform.

Defining Purpose and Scope

In the first two weeks, we defined the app's purpose, focusing on travelers with work permits rather than the general traveler population. This initial phase set the foundation for our design and development process.

Survey Creation and Analysis

We created a questionnaire and conducted a survey with relevant groups to gather insights into the needs and preferences of our target users. After collecting survey results, we moved on to competitor analysis.

Competitor Analysis

We analyzed current market competitors to understand how they address similar user needs and to identify gaps. This analysis informed our design strategy and helped us pinpoint areas where we could offer unique value.

Persona Development and User Journals

Based on the survey data, we developed personas that accurately represent our target users. We used these personas to guide our design decisions. We also implemented user journals to gain deeper insights into users' daily experiences and interactions with travel apps, which provided valuable context for our design.

Site Map Creation

Using insights from the user journals, we created a site map to structure the app's content and functionality.

Low-Fi Design

We began by brainstorming design inspirations and creating low-fidelity wireframes. Through several iterative meetings, we refined these wireframes into our final low-fi version. Additionally, we also vote to select the app's name and primary/secondary colors at this stage.

High-Fi Design and Prototyping

We proceeded to develop the high-fidelity design and interactive prototype and the full prototype is presented in the slide photo at the beginning of this case study page.
